# Algebra Review

## Exponents and Powers

An **exponent** indicates how many times a base is multiplied by itself:

$$a^n = \underbrace{a \cdot a \cdot a \cdots a}_{n \text{ times}}$$

### Key Rules

| Rule | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Product | $a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$ | $2^3 \cdot 2^2 = 2^5 = 32$ |
| Quotient | $a^m / a^n = a^{m-n}$ | $5^4 / 5^2 = 5^2 = 25$ |
| Power of power | $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$ | $(3^2)^3 = 3^6 = 729$ |
| Zero exponent | $a^0 = 1$ | $7^0 = 1$ |
| Negative exponent | $a^{-n} = 1/a^n$ | $2^{-3} = 1/8$ |

## Linear Equations

A **linear equation** in one variable has the form $ax + b = 0$, solved as:

$$x = -\frac{b}{a} \quad (a \neq 0)$$

### Example

Solve $3x - 9 = 0$:

$$3x = 9 \implies x = \frac{9}{3} = 3$$

## Quadratic Equations

The general form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ is solved using the **quadratic formula**:

$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

The **discriminant** $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$ determines the number of real roots:

- $\Delta > 0$ → two distinct real roots
- $\Delta = 0$ → one repeated real root
- $\Delta < 0$ → no real roots (complex roots only)
